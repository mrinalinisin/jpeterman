# Moroccan Coat

Beneath the Lanterns.
 "To visit Morocco is still like turning the pages of some illuminated Persian manuscript all embroidered with bright shapes and subtle lines."

- Edith Wharton

 

You wore this to wander through the souks of Marrakech, the rich aromas of spices full in your head. (Shopped well and sent home a crate of pottery and rugs.)

It was a dressed-up, comfortable layer for the flight home, too. Easy to wear over leggings or trousers. Jeans, too. 
 Striking colors. Wonderful drape. 
 You're not sure you ever really came home, so you put this on and think on the question.
 How can simple lines create such complicated feelings?
 A question as old as time.

 

Moroccan Coat (No. J7434). An open-front top layer as simple as it is rich in details. The drama of a neck-to-ankle coat, its effect amplified by contrasting embroidery at neck, cuffs, and center front. 100% cotton. Unlined. Imported.
