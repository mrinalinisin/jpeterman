One Pattern. Endless Interpretations.

Leopard.

Irresistible. Dangerous. Playful.

Lithe. Sophisticated.

Powerful. Splendid.

The chicest women have even come to think of leopard as a neutral, in function, if not in spirit. Looks marvelous with black or gold or brown. With scarlet, cobalt, emerald. Anchors an ensemble in a way no other pattern can.

First seen on the runway in 1947. Later on the character of Helen St. James in The Lost Weekend. But never against the wall, merely observing.

And yet. The ruffle indicates something demure.

The combination is devastating. Confounding. Magnificent.

But neutral? Only in the service of your grand plans.
