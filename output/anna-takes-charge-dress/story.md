# Anna Takes Charge Dress

A Few Miles Outside Missoula.

 

Anna stands at the fence, arms propped upon it, her eyes locked on rider and horse as they come to an understanding.

Buck, hold, whoa, settle. Steady, steady.

She would be in the ring herself, if not for two hundred guests and a champagne fundraiser in the barn. Fiddle sounds float across the corral.

Here she is, dressed for the evening in delicious tiers of drifting cotton voile. Miles of paisley floral print. Hair done up. Boots, polished. Turquoise and silver earrings dangle deliciously.

But, this. Synapses fire with the urge to take her place on the horse. The party can wait.

She signals to the rider, opens the gate.
